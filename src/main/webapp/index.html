COOKIES

Cookies, also known as HTTP cookies, are small text files that are stored on your web browser when you visit a web site.

They contain bits of data such as a user name and password, which are used to identify your computer as you use a network.

Here are some key points about cookies:

  • Purpose: Cookies are used by websites to track a user's journey, enabling them to offer features that are specific to each individual user. They help web developers give you a more personal and convenient website visit. For example, they let websites remember you, your logins, shopping carts, and more...
  • Types of Cookies: There are two types of cookies
    1. Authentication Cookies: These save user's information when they log into a website. This includes username and password, which account they're using and whether they're currently logged in.
    2. Tracking Cookies: These record your web usage and save information about your session, i.e., your time on a website. They track pages you visit and personalize your searches, displaying cusotm content tailored to your interests.
  • Privacy Concerns: While most cookies are perfectly safe, some can be used to track you without your consent by cybercriminals. Advertising - related tracking cookies can be placed on your device in two ways, enven if you don't interact with the ads on a website. Due to international laws, many websites are now required to ask for permsission to use certain cookies with your browser and prodive you with information on how their cookies will be used if you accept.

Remember, while cookies are essential for a modern internet experience, they can also be a serious vulnerability to your privacy. Therefore, it's important to manage your cookies settings according to your comfort level with sharing your information.

FILTER

In Java Web Application, a filter is an object that performs filtering tasks on either the request to a resource (a servlet or static content), or on the response from a resource or both.

Filters are define and then mapped to a string or set of strings that describe a request URL pattern. They are used for a variety of purposes including:

  • Logging: They can log request parameters to log files.
  • Authentication an d Authorization: They can check if a user has the right to access certain resources.
  • Formatting: They can modify the request body or headers before it's sent to a servlet.
  • Compression: They can compress the response data sent to the client.
  • Modification: They can alter the response by adding cookies header information, etc.
